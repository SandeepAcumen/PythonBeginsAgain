Q22. What is difference between Data Lake and Delta Lake?

Data Lake:
-Stores raw data (structured, semi-structured, unstructured)
-No built-in support for ACID transactions
-Data quality and consistency issues can occur
-Slower performance for updates and queries

Delta Lake:
-Built on top of a Data Lake (adds a storage layer)
-Supports ACID transactions (reliable & consistent data)
-Handles schema enforcement and evolution
-Provides faster queries with optimizations (like indexing, caching)
-Supports time travel (versioning of data)    //code - " like version as of 1 "

Q26. Do you know about predicate push down?
    👉 Predicate pushdown is an optimization technique where filter conditions (WHERE clause) are pushed down 
    to the data source, so only required data is read instead of the entire dataset.

    ✅ With pushdown: Only rows where country = 'India' are read from source

Q28. When you submit the spark job ,when data Frame is created.. what happend after that ?

After creating a DataFrame, Spark builds a logical plan but does not execute it immediately due to lazy evaluation. 
Once an action is triggered, Spark optimizes the plan using the Catalyst optimizer, converts it into a physical plan,
 and executes it as a DAG across the cluster.

Q30. why dataframe is considerd as immutable??

A DataFrame is immutable because once it is created, its data cannot be changed. Any transformation on a 
DataFrame returns a new DataFrame instead of modifying the original one.
